What is body fat percentage?

Body fat percentage is the proportion of fat mass relative to total body weight. Unlike BMI, which only considers height and weight, body fat percentage gives a clearer picture of fitness and health. Lean body mass includes muscles, bones, organs, and water — everything except fat. Tracking body fat helps assess metabolic health, athletic performance, and risk for chronic diseases.

BMI vs Body Fat: the difference

BMI (body mass index) often misclassifies muscular individuals as overweight. Body fat measurement distinguishes fat from muscle. Our AI tool uses the US Navy formula, a proven method used by militaries and fitness professionals.

How is body fat measured?

Common methods: skinfold calipers, bioelectrical impedance, DEXA scans, and the Navy circumference method. The Navy formula (used here) requires neck, waist, and hip (for women) circumferences, and correlates highly with DEXA. It’s affordable, fast, and accurate enough for most people.

Who should use this tool?

Lean body mass (LBM) is your total weight minus fat weight. Increasing LBM boosts metabolism. The tool calculates both fat mass and LBM instantly.

Navy formula explained: For men: 86.010 × log10(waist – neck) – 70.041 × log10(height) + 36.76. For women: 163.205 × log10(waist + hip – neck) – 97.684 × log10(height) – 78.387. All units are in inches; our script converts metric to inches behind the scenes.
